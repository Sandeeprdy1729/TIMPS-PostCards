Oracle Cuts Up to 30,000 Jobs to Fund the $500B Stargate Buildout

The clearest evidence yet that the AI infrastructure boom is being financed by cuts to the human workforce that built the old cloud business.
30K Jobs Cut

Oracle is eliminating up to 30,000 positions as it redirects capital toward its share of the $500 billion Stargate AI infrastructure partnership with OpenAI, Microsoft, and SoftBank. The cuts span legacy cloud, support, and administrative roles — the clearest signal yet that the AI hardware buildout is being funded, in part, by dismantling the workforce that ran the pre-AI business.

The move lands the same week TSMC posted record profit on AI chip demand, underlining a pattern across the industry in 2026: capital keeps flowing to compute, even as headcount in adjacent divisions shrinks.

Big Tech · Security

Microsoft Builds a Multi-Model Rival to Anthropic's Mythos Tier

Project Perception blends Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic models into one cybersecurity product — timed to a moment when Anthropic's own top-tier model faced export restrictions.

Microsoft is preparing to release Project Perception, an AI cybersecurity tool that selects across Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic models depending on the task. It's being positioned internally as a cheaper alternative to Anthropic's Mythos-tier models, with the kind of global distribution reach Anthropic can't yet match on its own.

The timing is notable: the launch comes in the same stretch where Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 access was briefly suspended under U.S. export controls before being restored on July 1 — a reminder that regulatory friction at one lab can open a window for a rival.

03 · MODELS
Model Race · Google

Gemini 3.5 Pro Misses Its Target a Third Time

Google is now weighing a stopgap "Gemini 3.6 Flash" release after internal testing found coding and long-horizon reasoning fell short of expectations.

Alphabet has delayed the broader rollout of Gemini 3.5 Pro for a third time, after internal evaluations showed the model underperforming on coding and complex, long-horizon reasoning tasks relative to what the company had promised at Google I/O. The model remains in limited enterprise preview while engineers keep iterating.

Google is reportedly exploring a smaller "Gemini 3.6 Flash" as a stopgap release to keep pace with Anthropic and OpenAI while the flagship model gets rebuilt. Alphabet shares fell more than 4% following the news, reflecting investor unease about Google's AI execution timeline.

04 · HARDWARE
Hardware · Earnings

TSMC's Record Quarter Confirms the AI Chip Boom Is Real

Net income up 77% year-on-year, AI chips now two-thirds of quarterly revenue, and another $100 billion pledged for Arizona.
+77% Q2 Net Income YoY

TSMC's Q2 2026 results show net income up 77.4% year-on-year, with high-performance computing — the AI chip segment — now making up roughly two-thirds of quarterly revenue. The company raised its full-year revenue guidance to 40%+ growth and committed another $100 billion to its Arizona expansion.

Because nearly every advanced AI processor on Earth routes through a small number of Taiwanese fabs, the number matters beyond Wall Street: it converts every hyperscaler's compute pledge from an announcement into an actual wafer order.

05 · GEOPOLITICS
Geopolitics · Policy

Xi Jinping Launches a New Global AI Alliance

At Shanghai's World AI Conference, China proposed the WAICO alliance and urged the world not to let any one country dominate AI — a pointed message aimed at Washington.

Chinese President Xi Jinping used the World AI Conference in Shanghai to unveil the World AI Cooperation Organization (WAICO), calling on countries to cooperate on AI governance and warning against any single nation dominating the technology. Analysts read it as Beijing positioning itself to shape global AI rules as the US-China tech rivalry sharpens.

The announcement lands alongside China showcasing domestic hardware and model progress meant to signal parity with the US — part of a broader pattern this month of both powers treating AI capability as a geopolitical asset, not just a commercial one.
