Top Story of the Week
The $45 Billion Compute Reckoning: OpenAI, SpaceX & The New AI Finance Era
A near-$45B Anthropic–SpaceX compute arrangement, dual IPO filings, and Microsoft's Maia chip deal are redrawing the map of who controls AI's future infrastructure.

The AI industry is entering what observers are calling its IPO era. OpenAI is advancing preparations for a public listing while SpaceX simultaneously moves toward a large-scale filing — two of the most closely watched private companies heading to markets in the same window.

The backdrop: a reported ~$45B compute arrangement between Anthropic and SpaceX. Analysis of SpaceX's IPO disclosures points directly to how compute is now the primary bottleneck and strategic asset. NVIDIA has identified a new large market opportunity tied to this infrastructure build-out.

Separately, Microsoft will supply its custom Maia silicon to Anthropic — a direct answer to mounting GPU scarcity. Cursor has crossed an estimated $3B ARR, signalling AI-native developer tools are past the early-adopter phase. For builders and investors, hardware is the new moat — not the models themselves.
