OpenAI Ships GPT-5.6 — and an Agent That Wants Your Whole Job

Three durable tiers — Sol, Terra, Luna — go public alongside ChatGPT Work, an agent built to finish tasks rather than just answer them.
54% More token-efficient on agentic coding

On July 9, OpenAI moved GPT-5.6 from limited government-vetted preview to full public release, retiring the old "mini/nano" naming in favour of three durable capability tiers. Sol is the flagship for hard, long-running reasoning; Terra matches GPT-5.5 at half the cost; Luna is the fast, cheap workhorse — priced at $5/$30, $2.50/$15 and $1/$6 per million tokens respectively.

Sam Altman told CNBC that Sol is 54% more token-efficient on agentic coding, and OpenAI is pairing the launch with ChatGPT Work — an agent that folds Codex into a single desktop app to handle documents, spreadsheets and multi-step business tasks with minimal oversight. The rollout followed a White House-linked review process; a White House official told CNBC the administration gave no formal "green light," leaving release decisions with OpenAI itself.

SK Hynix Raises $26.5B in the Largest Foreign Listing in US History

The world's top HBM chipmaker debuts on Nasdaq, opening 15% above its offer price and briefly touching a $1.25T valuation.
$26.5B Raised in ADR sale

SK Hynix priced 177.9 million ADRs at $149 each on Thursday, surpassing Alibaba's 2014 record to become the largest U.S. share sale ever by a foreign company. Shares opened Friday at roughly $170–$171 on the provisional ticker SKHYV, a gain of about 14–15%, pushing market cap above $1.2 trillion. Demand reportedly outstripped supply more than seven to one.

SK Hynix holds around 56% of the global high-bandwidth memory market and supplies Nvidia directly — the listing gives U.S. investors their first direct route into the world's dominant HBM producer, and analysts expect it to narrow the valuation gap with Micron.

Model Pricing War

Meta Undercuts Everyone With Muse Spark 1.1

Zuckerberg's first paid API model prices agentic coding at a fraction of OpenAI and Anthropic's rates.

Muse Spark 1.1 launched in U.S. public preview at $1.25 input / $4.25 output per million tokens — well below GPT-5.5's $5/$30 and Claude Opus 4.8's $5/$25. It's a reasoning model tuned for repository-level coding, computer-use tasks, and multi-agent orchestration, with a 1M-token context window and a $20 free-credit onboarding tier for new developers.

Analysts told InfoWorld the pricing is a "shot across the bow" that could force OpenAI and Anthropic to compete harder on inference economics, even as some expect Meta to raise prices 30–50% once market share solidifies.

Open Models

Ollama Raises $65M as Open-Weight Tooling Hits 9M Developers

A 14-person team now sits inside 85% of the Fortune 500 — and Benchmark says open weights may soon generate most AI tokens.

Ollama closed a $65 million Series B led by Theory Ventures, bringing total funding to $88 million. Founder Jeff Morgan says the tool — which lets developers run open-weight models locally in one command — now reaches 8.9 million developers monthly, up from half that in January, adding close to a million installs a week.

Benchmark's Peter Fenton, who joined Ollama's board after leading its Series A, argues open-weight models will generate the "supermajority" of AI tokens within 18–24 months — framing Ollama as the platform layer everything else plugs into.
