Frontier Models

Gemini 3.5 Pro Finally Lands — Six Weeks Late, Straight Into the Busiest Week AI Has Ever Had

Google scrapped the original base model and rebuilt from scratch. Today it ships into a market already holding GPT-5.6 and Grok 4.5.

Google DeepMind's Gemini 3.5 Pro is targeting general availability today, July 17 — after Google abandoned the 2.5 Pro architecture entirely and restarted pretraining from a native Gemini 3 foundation to close gaps in math reasoning and SVG generation.

Leaked specs point to a 2-million-token context window, a Deep Think reasoning mode gated behind the $250/month Ultra tier, and API pricing near $1.25 input / $10 output per million tokens — a fraction of GPT-5.6 Sol's rate with double the context.

The stakes: Google has spent the delay bleeding talent, with Noam Shazeer departing for OpenAI and Nobel laureate John Jumper leaving for Anthropic. A strong launch today quiets that story. Another slip does not.

02 · GOVERNANCE
Global Policy

Xi Jinping Makes His First-Ever Appearance at WAIC, Pitches AI as a "Symphony of Global Cooperation"

Beijing's flagship AI conference opened today in Shanghai with the country's president on stage for the first time since the event began in 2018.

Speaking at the opening of the 2026 World AI Conference, Xi said AI development "should not be a solo performance by any single country," and pledged to expand cooperation with ASEAN, the African Union, BRICS, and the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation.

A day earlier, 29 countries — including Pakistan, Russia, and Kazakhstan — signed on to establish a new World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization headquartered in Shanghai. More than 1,100 companies and 1,400 guests are attending the conference, which runs through July 20.

03 · HARDWARE
Infrastructure

Huawei Debuts the Atlas 950 SuperPoD on Home Turf, Taking Its Nvidia Challenge to Xi's Own Stage

The industry's largest publicly shown supernode — 8,192 Ascend NPUs acting as one machine — gets its first domestic public showing at WAIC.
8,192 NPUs per SuperPoD

Huawei is showcasing the Atlas 950 SuperPoD publicly in China for the first time, built on its proprietary UnifiedBus interconnect and rated up to 8 exaflops of FP8 performance across roughly 160 cabinets.

The timing is pointed: DeepSeek has said prices for its V4-Pro model should drop substantially once Ascend 950 nodes reach mass production later this year, and Alibaba Cloud is separately fielding its own rack-scale supernode — signalling China is racing to reduce dependence on Nvidia chips it can no longer freely access.

04 · MARKETS
Funding & Revenue

Anthropic and OpenAI Alone Soak Up 43% of a Record $510B Global VC Half-Year

Startup funding hit an all-time high in H1 2026 — and the story isn't spread, it's concentration.

Crunchbase's H1 2026 report puts global venture funding at a record $510 billion, with OpenAI and Anthropic together accounting for roughly $217 billion of it. PitchBook separately clocked US venture funding at $412.7 billion, up 30% on all of 2025, with AI startups capturing 86% of that total.

Fortune separately reports Anthropic has overtaken OpenAI on annualized revenue — about $47 billion versus $25–33 billion projected for OpenAI — with Claude Code alone growing from $1B to $2.5B+ in roughly two months earlier this year.

05 · SAFETY
Accountability

Frontier Labs Get Report Cards — Best Grade in the Class Is a C+

The Future of Life Institute's 2026 AI Safety Index found no lab earning better than a C+, with several effectively failing.

Anthropic topped the independent watchdog's 2026 AI Safety Index with a C+ — the highest score any frontier lab received. OpenAI and Google DeepMind landed at C, Meta at D+, and xAI, DeepSeek, and Mistral effectively failed the assessment.

The grading lands the same week South Korea committed $880 billion to AI development over the next decade and reports surfaced that Andrej Karpathy has joined Anthropic — underscoring an industry being scored, funded, and staffed simultaneously.
